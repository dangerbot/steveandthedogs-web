Proposal
Paywall · Quick Wins

Four edits we can ship without rebuilding.

01 · Lead with outcomes

Replace “52% savings” with what subscribers actually get.

“Grow 3× more food. Lose fewer plants. Have Kelby in your pocket.” Pricing comes after the promise — not before it.

02 · Drop the locks

Show benefits as benefits, not as forbidden territory.

Replace the 🔒 column with checkmarks and short descriptions. The ask is for value, not for permission.

03 · Reframe the trial

“Try Kelby free for 14 days” — not “$0 due today, renews at $39.”

Make the trial feel like a gift, not a billing event. Move the renewal disclosure to a single, honest line below the CTA.

04 · Make declining graceful

Move “Continue without membership” up — and remove the guilt.

Today the only way out is a tiny “No Thanks” at the bottom. A confident product offers an exit at eye level — and earns the upgrade later.

Concept
A Bigger Bet — For Discussion

Credits, not locks.

The quick wins fix the framing. The bigger question: should the free tier feel like a different product, or the same product with a daily budget?
Today — Lock & list
Two products: a free shell, and a paid app. The free user never meets Kelby.
Free
Support & replacements, warranty — the reactive stuff.
Locked 🔒
Kelby, personalization, discovery, community — everything compelling.
The implicit message: “Here's everything you can't have.”
Proposed — One Kelby, metered
Don't build two Kelbys. Build one great Kelby and meter the access.
Every user gets the real Kelby
Same personality. Same intelligence. Same garden awareness.
FREE · CREDITS MEMBERS · UNLIMITED
Free
Morning check-in, garden status, tips, celebrations. When you come back for more: a gentle nudge to upgrade.
Members
Ask anything, anytime. Proactive alerts. Vacation mode, time-lapse, yield tracking, community.
Borrowed from how AI products like Claude or ChatGPT shape their free tiers — same product, daily budget. Worth exploring; not a tomorrow decision.
Let every user experience the real Kelby. When they hit the limit, the upgrade sells itself.

Recommendations
Recommendations · Near-term

Quick wins.

01 · Advocacy

Turn silent fans into vocal advocates.

Gardyn's 4.8-star rating proves the happy users are there — they just aren't telling the story. Prompt written reviews at milestone moments: first harvest, growth streaks, Kelby celebrations.

02 · Feedback loop

Capture frustration privately, before the App Store does.

An in-app “Having trouble? Tell us directly” channel intercepts the venting and keeps it out of a 1-star review.

03 · Polish

Kill cookie consent inside native web views.

Small change, outsized perception hit. A quick fix for perceived quality — and it's free.

04 · Discovery

Show what subscribing actually feels like.

Inside the free app, sprinkle real previews of Kelby — a sample morning check-in, a glimpse of the Journal, a celebrated harvest. Make membership a place users have already visited.

Recommendations
Recommendations · Longer-horizon

Bigger bets.

05 · Paywall

Redesign the paywall as a value preview, not a gate.

Let users taste Kelby before they pay. A free tier that gives you the real Kelby with a daily budget. Make the free experience so good that upgrading feels obvious, not forced.

06 · Retention

Make Kelby the retention engine.

Build lifecycle intelligence into Kelby. Detect when a Gardyner goes quiet and have Kelby intervene with personal nudges before disengagement becomes churn. Kelby shouldn't just celebrate wins — Kelby should prevent losses.

07 · First run

Design for the first win.

Get users to a moment of delight before asking for anything. First plant scan, first Kelby greeting, first growth milestone. The first ten minutes should end with “this is actually cool.”

08 · Habit

Build daily habits through community and gamification.

Harvest streaks, seasonal challenges, recipe sharing, Gardyner leaderboards. Make checking Kelby feel as natural as checking the weather. A Strava for home growing.
